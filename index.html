ECOSTRESS Tutorials

The ECOSTRESS tutorials are a set of written and video tutorials designed to guide users through the very basics of installing software, to downloading data, and applying it with some simple codes. This aligns with NASA’s Transform to Open Science (TOPS) goal of broadening science to a wider audience and lowers the barrier to entry for those interested in accessing ECOSTRESS data. The tutorials have a written and video version to accommodate a variety of learning styles. Some tutorials also have associated code that is stored in the ECOSTRESS tutorials GitHub, where we invite users to provide suggestions on how it can be improved. Additionally, I developed a template and workflow for these tutorials so that other ECOSTRESS employees can make their own tutorials based on the work they are doing or as a response to the needs of the working group.

Calculating Spatial Accessibility to Primary Healthcare Providers in Yucatán, Mexico

This project examined spatial accessibility to primary healthcare facilities in the state of Yucatán, Mexico by employing the Two-Step Floating Catchment Area (2SFCA) method. Access to primary healthcare plays an important role in enhancing overall health outcomes, especially due to preventative care measures. 2SFCA was used to first calculate a provider-to-population ratio using network analysis, and then aggregate these ratios within population catchment areas. The end result is a spatial accessibility index, which is a valuable metric to evaluate relative spatial accessibility of population points to primary healthcare locations. This research helps quantify accessibility, with potential applications aiding in policy development and service planning, ultimately resulting in improved health outcomes for the population.

Quantifying Potential Spatial Accessibility to Adult American Sign Language Classes in Los Angeles County

This research examines potential spatial accessibility to adult American Sign Language (ASL) learning opportunities in Los Angeles County by employing the Two-Step Floating Catchment Area (2SFCA) method. Over 90% of all deaf children are born to hearing parents, who typically do not know sign language. Research has shown that deaf children whose parents learn sign language tend to have better outcomes than those who do not. Thus, adult accessibility to sign language classes is crucial for the success of deaf children. The findings show high accessibility in Lancaster-Palmdale, the San Fernando Valley, and Long Beach, while showing low accessibility in the Santa Monica Mountains and Central Los Angeles.

Southern California Water Resources - Using NASA Earth Observations to Monitor Seagrass Extent and Water Quality Parameters

This project investigated water quality parameters associated with eelgrass in Newport Bay and Mission Bay, California. Using satellite data from Landsat 8 & 9, Sentinel-2, and ECOSTRESS, we created time series of turbidity, sea surface temperature, and chlorophyll-a concentration from 2019–2023. These data revealed cyclical seasonal trends, with peaks in turbidity and temperature in the summer. This research helps support ecosystem monitoring efforts.
